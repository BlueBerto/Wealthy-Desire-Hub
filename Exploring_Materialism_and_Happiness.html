Status

It is believed by many people that they could attain a sense of recognition or achievement from using and owning the right kind of product. This can be an important motivator of behavior. Materialism basically lies on the ownership and acquisition of items that are aligned in achieving major life goals. A person’s status may increase consumption and can motivate behavior.

EXAMPLE

In certain societies, jewelry symbolizes social status and prosperity, making it a visible indicator of one's wealth and desirability. This belief underscores the cultural significance placed on outward displays of affluence, where expensive jewelry is seen not just as adornment but as a strategic statement in attracting potential suitors. Historically, lavish accessories have been intertwined with notions of power and allure, shaping perceptions of beauty and success in relationships.
